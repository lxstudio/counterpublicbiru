About Us

Novia

Novia holds a doctorate degree in Gender, Sexuality and Women's Studies and a Master of Fine Arts degree in Film Production. Her research interests fall under the broader umbrella of Gender and Media that draws from various disciplines including feminist film theory and criticism, documentary film studies, Sinophone studies, and cultural studies. Novia is also a Domestic Violence Outreach Worker at a local society where she intends to bridge theory and practice while developing her community engagement skills, activism, and organizing capacity.

Carmen

Carmen is a communications designer with backgrounds in branding, web technologies, generative visuals, and theatrical lighting. With a passion in visual culture and media analysis, Carmen is an advocate of visual literacy and she strives to explore diverse modes of creative expressions and embrace the dualities of art and tech, aesthetics and functions, in her practices. As an immigrant, Carmen is critical of the systemic oppression in racial and gender equality, technological prejudice in relation to freedom of expression and access to information, as well as the exploitation of labour and environment in our society.

Hope B.C.

Our beers are brewed in the traditional, ancestral and unceded territory of the Chawathil First Nation.

We acknowledge that we are minority settlers of Turtle Island, colonially known as Canada. We are committed to revealing truths through respectful conversations and ongoing education.

Aged in oak, blended, and bottle conditioned, our beers are brewed at Mountaiview Brewing Co. in Hope, British Columbia, Canada.
